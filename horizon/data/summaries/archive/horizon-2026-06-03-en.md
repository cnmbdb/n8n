## [Elixir 1.20 Introduces Gradual Typing](https://elixir-lang.org/blog/2026/06/03/elixir-v1-20-0-released/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Elixir 1.20, released on June 3, 2026, introduces a gradual type system, allowing developers to optionally add static type annotations to their code while retaining the dynamic nature of the language. This marks a major evolution for Elixir, enabling earlier bug detection and better tooling without sacrificing the flexibility that made it popular. It could attract more developers from statically-typed languages and strengthen Elixir's position in production systems. The gradual type system is opt-in, meaning existing untyped code runs unchanged. Early community reports indicate faster compilation times in large projects, while performance of typed code may vary depending on type discipline.

hackernews · cloud8421 · Jun 3, 19:02 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48388324)

**Background**: Gradual typing is a type system that allows mixing static and dynamic typing within the same language, letting programmers add type annotations where desired while leaving other parts dynamically typed. This approach has been implemented in languages like Racket and TypeScript, and aims to combine the safety of static types with the flexibility of dynamic languages.

## [Chinese scientists discover RGF gene to combat crop cold damage](https://www.ithome.com/0/959/578.htm) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers led by Xu Cao at the Institute of Genetics and Developmental Biology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, discovered the small peptide gene RGF that activates only under cold stress during crop flowering, recovering over 50% of yield loss. The study was published online in Nature on June 3, 2026. This breakthrough solves the long-standing dilemma that crops cannot simultaneously achieve high yield and cold tolerance, reducing yield losses from sudden cold spells like 'late spring cold' and 'early frost'. It provides a new approach for global food security and expanding crop cultivation areas. RGF encodes a 13-amino-acid peptide that is nearly silent under normal temperatures but rapidly activates in anther tapetum cells during the tetrad stage of pollen development under cold stress. Field tests showed RGF recovered 33.9%–52.2% yield loss in tomato and about 18% in rice, without affecting normal yield.

rss · IT之家 · Jun 3, 15:35

**Background**: Crop flowering is extremely sensitive to cold, with a few days of low temperature causing 20%–60% yield loss in staple crops. Traditional passive defenses (irrigation, smoking, covering) are costly and ineffective, while constitutive cold-resistance genes waste energy, leading to the 'high yield vs. cold tolerance' trade-off.
